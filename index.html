Profile

My name is Hiyab Woldegebriel, and I am an aspiring Full Stack Developer. Though I am currently pursuing a Bachelors in Computer Science with Business Applications, most of my knowledge regarding front-end and back-end material is self taught.

Art is one of my greatest passions; in fact, this is what originally attracted me towards full stack development (particularly the front-end aspect of it). As I grew older and progressed in my college career, I later began exploring back-end related content, and decided that I thought it was interesting as well.



My Story

Many that know me would identify me as a leader who speaks up for what is right. Being a black woman growing up in a predominantly white city, I was constantly belittled and my achievements were often overlooked. The only validation I would ever recieve came from my close friends and family, which would fuel my imposter syndrome and led me to believe that I would never make it as an engineer. I learned that I was not alone in my struggles when I entered highschool, and that many other people of color share similar experiences as mine. By my sophomore year of high school, I was founder and president of the Pro-Diversity Club, a safe space for people to discuss the injustices minorities face in society. Despite the overwhelming amount of planning and dedication it took to run the club, as well as the continual backlash of racist staff and peers, my club was a success. Many of my peers would come up to me and personally thank me for creating an outlet for them to talk about their hardships and recieve comfort from like-minded individuals, something I wish I had growing up but am happy to give to others.

Now, I recognize that my identity is what makes me special. I am grateful for the obstacles I've encountered because it is what fuels me to do better and be better. I learned to be independent, communicative, and hardworking - all of which are reflected in my work ethic. I have learned how to be self sufficient and learn challenging concepts on my own. I am also able to manage my time accordingly and finish tasks long before its deadline. I work ten times harder and finish ten times faster than my peers, because I am driven to prove people wrong about me.

Front-End Development Intern at ThinqTv


As of late July of 2020, I began my journey working as an intern for Thinq TV, a web application dedicated to promoting important conversations about the struggles of women and minorities in modern society. I was primarily responsible for either constructing from scratch or redesigning their wepbages, which required an extensive knowledge of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Ruby on Rails. I often work with a partner, which required effective collaboration and communication in order to get work done efficiently.
Working at ThinqTv has taught me to be self sufficient and figure out problems I would run into on my own rather than always seeking for help (though if it is necessary, I have no problem reaching out to my coworkers). I have also learned how important it is to me to work somewhere that makes me genuinely feel like I'm making a positive impact on society. Occasionally, I would participate in UI/UX or backend tasks, which familiarized me with softwares like Figma and systems such as Postgres.
